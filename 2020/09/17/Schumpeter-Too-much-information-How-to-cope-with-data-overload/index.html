Schumpeter-Too much information-How to cope with data overload

学习The Economist系列文章

文章整理自Soren，仅作个人学习使用，不作任何商业用途。

Schumpeter: Too much information How to cope with data overload

Schumpeter是这篇文章的专栏名，翻译为“熊彼特”。这个名称来自提出了“创新理论”的经济学家约瑟夫·熊彼特。该理论用一句话概括就是：创新的本质，就是创造性破坏（creative destruction）。换言之，创新同时意味着毁灭。

Google “information overload” and you are immediately overloaded with information: more than 7m hits in 0.05 seconds.

  • be overloaded with… 相当于 be given too much of sth 或 be burdened with…，可以用来形容「某物很多」的情形，比如现在到处都是广告，我们可以说： Consumers are now overloaded with advertising.
    也可以用be overloaded形容人的工作繁重：Those in the IT sector are often overloaded with work.
  • hit 此处指在计算机或互联网上搜索的查询结果，也可以用result替换。

Some of this information is interesting: for example, that the phrase “information overload” was popularised by Alvin Toffler in 1970. Some of it is mere noise: obscure companies promoting their services and even more obscure bloggers sounding off.

  • sound off 表示：If someone sounds off, they express their opinions strongly and rather rudely without being asked. 在未经邀请的情况下，强烈、粗鲁地表达自己的观点。指的就是那些装逼吐槽的键盘侠们。

The overall impression is at once overwhelming and confusing.

  • the overall impression = the general impression 表示”整体印象“。

“Information overload” is one of the biggest irritations in modern life.

  • irritation作不可数名词时，表示抽象含义“恼怒、生气”，相当于annoyance/anger；当作可数名词时，表示具体含义“令人生气的事”（something that makes you angry）。
    所以文中 one of the biggest irritations in modern life 就可以理解成：当代生活中最令人恼怒的事情之一。

There are e-mails to answer, virtual friends to pester, YouTube videos to watch and, back in the physical world, meetings to attend, papers to shuffle and spouses to appease.

  • pester 在这里是keep talking to的意思（以至于让对方有点厌烦），可以翻译成“纠缠、不断叨扰”。
  • shuffle 可以表示to move paper or things into different positions or a different order，文中 papers to shuffle 可以理解成“整理文件”。

A survey by Reuters once found that two-thirds of managers believe that the data deluge has made their jobs less satisfying or hurt their personal relationship. One-third think that it has damaged their health.

  • deluge 表示“暴雨、洪水”，常比喻像洪水一样多的东西
    比如：

    • Viewers sent a deluge of complaints about the show.
      观众对这档节目的投诉如潮水般涌来。
    • This has brought a deluge of criticism.
      这一下子招来了铺天盖地的批评。

    文中data deluge就是“海量数据”的意思，和上文的overload information是同义替换。

  • made their jobs less satisfying 的字面解释是：让他们的工作没有那么令人满意。但我们翻译时可转化成动词：降低工作的满意度。更符合汉语表述。

Another survey suggests that most managers think most of the information they receive is useless.

Commentators have coined a profusion of phrases to describe the anxiety and anomie caused by too much information: “data asphyxiation” (William van Winkle), “data smog” (David Shenk), “information fatigue syndrome” (David Lewis), “cognitive overload” (Eric Schmidt) and “time famine” (Leslie Perlow).

时事评论员们创造了众多新词，来形容“信息过载”所导致的焦虑和失序，例如：威廉·范·维克创造的“数据窒息”、大卫·申克创造的“数据迷雾”、大卫·路易斯创造的“信息疲劳综合征”、埃里克·施密特创造的“认知超载”，以及莱斯利·佩罗创造的“时间饥荒”。

  • coin 本义是名词“硬币”，作动词则表示“铸币、造硬币”，进而引申出“造新词”（ invent a new word or phrase）。
  • profusion 表示“大量”，a profusion of等于a lot of。

Johann Hari, a British journalist, notes that there is a good reason why “wired” means both “connected to the internet” and “high, frantic, unable to concentrate”.

英国记者约翰•哈里表示，目前有充分的理由能说明，“wired”一词不仅表示“连接互联网”，还指“亢奋、疯狂、无法集中注意力”。

These worries are exaggerated.

这些担忧显然言过其实。

Stick-in-the-muds have always complained about new technologies: the Victorians fussed that the telegraph meant that “the businessman of the present day must be continually on the jump”.

墨守成规的人总会抱怨新科技，正如维多利亚时代的人曾大惊小怪地认为，电报的出现意味着“如今的商人必须要忙里忙外”。

  • stick-in-the-muds 字面意思是“插在泥里”，如果一根棍子插在泥里，很难挪动，所以文中stick-in-the-muds实际指“顽固不化者，不懂变通的人”，可以用stubborn, obstinate, die-hard等形容词替换。

  • on the jump字面意思是“跳来跳去、在跳跃中”，比喻 moving, happening, or occurring at a very fast or hurried pace “迅速的、飞快的”，形容节奏之快，举个例子：

    • During the economic boom, buildings were being built on the jump.
      经济繁荣时期，高楼大厦拔地而起。

    所以文中，the businessman of the present day must be continually on the jump 的意思是：如今的商人必须不断地忙里忙外。

And businesspeople have always had to deal with constant pressure and interruptions - hence the word “business”.

  • hence后面的内容，可以是句子的形式，也可以是词的结构，例如：
    • The cost of transport is a major expense for an industry. Hence factory location is an important consideration.
      运输成本是企业的一大支出，因此工厂选址是重要的考虑因素。
    • The Trump administration is profoundly divided and hence very weak.
      特朗普政府内部分歧严重，因此非常脆弱。
    • We suspect they are trying to hide something, hence the need for an independent inquiry.
      我们怀疑他们在企图隐瞒什么事，因此有必要进行独立调查。

In his classic study of managerial work in 1973 Henry Mintzberg compared managers to jugglers: they keep 50 balls in the air and periodically check on each one before sending it aloft once more.

1973年，亨利·明茨伯格对他的管理工作做了一次经典研究，他把管理者比作一名正在接抛球的杂技演员——他要保证总有50个球在空中，并要时不时地确认每一个球的位置，之后再抛回空中。

  • compare… to… & compare… with… （A is compared with B when you draw attention to the difference. A is compared to B when you want to stress their similarity.）
    比如说：
    • Business activity has grown by 2% compared with the previous month.
      和上个月相比，商业活动增长了2%。

Yet clearly there is a problem. It is not merely the dizzying increase in the volume of information (the amount of data being stored doubles every 18 months). It is also the combination of omnipresence and fragmentation.

  • combination 我们都知道是“组合”的意思，但它常表示并列关系，可以翻译成“既…又…、以及”，比如文中the combination of omnipresence and fragmentation不要翻译成“（信息）无处不在和碎片化的结合”，而要说“无处不在的信息以及碎片化信息”。
    比如，2017年《经济学人》中有篇文章讲到了中国前央行行长周小川：

    • He has been central-bank governor for 15 years, the only ministerial-level official to straddle three administrations. He was due for retirement at the last big government reshuffle five years ago but was kept on, a credit to his rare combination of financial knowledge and political skills.
      他担任央行行长已有15年，是唯一一位跨越三届政府的部长级官员。在五年前的上一次政府大改组中，他本应退休，但却被留任，这要归功于很少有人像他这样，既有金融知识，也有政治技能。

    再比如，2020年1月的《经济学人》中有篇文章讲到了中国青少年的近视问题，文中有这么一句话：

    • The combination of an exam-crazed culture with the rapid spread of smartphones and computer-game technology explains much of China’s problem.
      疯狂的考试文化，以及智能手机和电脑游戏技术的迅速普及，是中国近视高发的主要原因。

Many professionals are welded to their smartphones.

  • weld 在这里是个很有新意的用法，它本义是“焊接、熔接”，这里用be welded to形容人们对手机的依赖——就像把人和手机焊接在一起似的。be welded to比be addicted to更有表现力。
    2019年《经济学人》中一篇“韩国低头族引发交通安全风险”的文章，里面有这么一句话：
    IN South Korea smartphone cases come with rings mounted on the back, to prevent clumsy owners from dropping them. This makes people look like they are literally married to their phones.
    在韩国，人们会在手机壳背部装上指环，以防止失手把手机摔落。这让人们看起来简直就像和手机结婚一样。

They are also constantly bombarded with unrelated bits and pieces — a poke from a friend one moment, the latest Greek financial tragedy the next.

他们还经常被无关紧要的琐碎信息狂轰滥炸”，比如，一会一个朋友戳了自己一下，一会又看到最近发生的希腊金融危机的消息。

  • bombard 在这里是一个非常生动形象的用法，它本指“轰炸”，比如 bombard the Normandy coast 轰炸诺曼底海岸，这里比喻被大量的无关紧要的信息轰炸，画面感一下子就出来了。
    再比如，“手机被打爆了”就可以说：My phone has been bombarded with calls.
    新浪微博上有很多吐槽抱怨声音，我们可以说：We have always been bombarded with complaints on Sina Weibo.
  • bits and pieces 表示small objects or items of various kinds 零七碎八的东西，文中可以理解成“琐碎信息”。

The data fog is thickening at a time when companies are trying to squeeze ever more of their workers.

  • squeeze 它本义是指“挤入、榨取”，当表示“挤入”时，有点类似pack和cram，比如：squeeze/pack/cram six people in a car 往一辆车里塞了六个人；
    我们任务繁忙的时候就可以说：
    We managed to squeeze/cram a lot into a week.
    我们把很多事挤在一个星期里边做完了。
    当表示“榨取”时，我们可以说 squeeze the juice from a lemon 把柠檬汁从柠檬中榨出来，squeeze the water out of the wet clothes 把湿衣服里的水分拧干。由此squeeze引申出“榨取（好处）”的意思，比如文中，squeeze ever more out of their workers 就可以理解成：从员工身上榨取更多（利润）。

A survey in America by Spherion Staffing discovered that 53% of workers had been compelled to take on extra tasks since the recession started. This dismal trend may well continued — many companies remain reluctant to hire new people even as business picks up.

  • pick up 一词，重点在于“up”。up表示向上的方向，所以pick up就可以理解成improve “改善、提高”。
    • Industrial production is beginning to pick up.
      工业生产开始好转。

So there will be little respite from the dense data smog, which some researchers fear may be poisonous.
