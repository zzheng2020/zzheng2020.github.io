Speech is silver, silence is gold

学习The Economist系列文章

文章整理自Soren，仅作个人学习使用，不作任何商业用途。

Speech is silver, silence is gold

  • Speech is silver, silence is gold(en) 是一个俗语，翻译过来是“能言是银，沉默是金”，用来表达“不说话比滔滔不绝或说错话更好”的意思。
    • silver tongue 指“铁齿铜牙”，比喻一个人能言善辩，用法为：sb has a silver tongue，或用其形容词形式：silver-tongued。

原文：

DONALD TRUMP and Joe Biden are not merely the two oldest candidates to contest a presidential election. They also may be the most prolix. Their speaking styles differ—Mr Trump riffs while Mr Biden rambles; Mr Trump nurses personal grievances and Mr Biden tells stories — but they both love to talk. However, in the run-up to last night’s debate, the campaign’s second and last, both campaigns signalled that they wanted their candidates to allow the other more speaking time.

Mr Trump’s campaign believed that the more Mr Biden spoke, the likelier he was to ramble and lose his train of thought, which would give credence to their charge that he has lost his mental acuity. Mr Biden’s camp—judging by the first debate’s aftermath, in which Mr Biden’s polling lead grew after Trump repeatedly interrupted, hectored and insulted him—reasoned that the more airtime Mr Trump gets, the less voters like him.

DONALD TRUMP and Joe Biden are not merely the two oldest candidates to contest a presidential election. They also may be the most prolix.

Their speaking styles differ—Mr Trump riffs while Mr Biden rambles; Mr Trump nurses personal grievances and Mr Biden tells stories — but they both love to talk.

他们讲话风格不同——特朗普喜欢重复，而拜登则东拉西扯；特朗普心怀不满，而拜登则讲述故事——但他们都有一个共同特点：能说。

  • riff 本是指爵士乐和流行音乐中的重复乐段，这里指特朗普讲话重复。
  • ramble 本义是“漫步、闲逛”，后面加介词再加地点，比如 ramble around this little island 漫步在这个小岛；文中指“瞎扯、闲聊”（像散步一样没有重点、漫无目的），用法为ramble (on) about + topic 东拉西扯地谈…，比如爷爷奶奶总喜欢唠唠叨叨讲过去的事情，就可以说：ramble on about old stories.
  • nurse 是作动词使用，我们可以由其名词“护士、保姆”大致推断出其动词含义——表示“怀抱、怀有”，后面可以加某种感觉或观点，比如：to nurse an ambition / a grievance / a grudge 心怀壮志 / 不满 / 怨恨，可以和harbour替换。

However, in the run-up to last night’s debate, the campaign’s second and last, both campaigns signalled that they wanted their candidates to allow the other more speaking time.

  • the run-up to… 是一个名词结构，指重大事件的“前期、准备阶段”，可以和the lead-up to…替换，
    • 比如，双十一临近，各大商家开始大量备货，可以说：
      In the lead-up to Singles Day, businesses have begun to stock up in large quantities.
  • signal 在这里是动词，表示express opinions，即“表示、表达”，both campaigns signalled that…就可以理解成：双方选举团队表示…

Mr Trump’s campaign believed that the more Mr Biden spoke, the likelier he was to ramble and lose his train of thought, which would give credence to their charge that he has lost his mental acuity.

特朗普的竞选团队认为，拜登讲的越多，就越有可能胡言乱语、失去思路，从而使他们的“拜登精神敏锐度下降”这一说法更加可信。

  • lose one’s train of thought 失去了思路的小火车，表示to forget what one was talking or thinking about 即“走神，失去思路”。

Mr Biden’s camp—judging by the first debate’s aftermath, in which Mr Biden’s polling lead grew after Trump repeatedly interrupted, hectored and insulted him—reasoned that the more airtime Mr Trump gets, the less voters like him.

从第一场辩论的结果来看，在特朗普反复打断、挑衅和侮辱拜登之后，拜登的民调领先优势进一步扩大。所以拜登团队认为，特朗普发言时间越多，选民就越不喜欢他。
