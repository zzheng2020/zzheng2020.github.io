如何表达人的情绪 / 感受

  • … come as …

    This may come as a surprise to those who have tried to make use of them in the past.

    e.g.

    • The recently exposed instances of academic dishonesty and imposture came as a great anger for the public.
      近期曝光的学术造假和冒名顶替的情况令人愤怒。
    • The rising delinquencies and the latest cases of fraud came as an anxiety for investors.

如何引出背景

  • 同样可以用 come as
    e.g.
    • The change came as a report from the Imperial College London’s COVID-19 Response Team suggested that without such measures, COVID-19 could kill half a million people in the UK.
    • The biggest economic stimulus plan not seen since 2008 came as the pandemic still rages.
      在疫情肆虐期间，美联储实施了08年后最大的经济刺激方案

如何进行对比

  • where, to say that one person, thing, opinion etc is different from another
    e.g.
    • Where others might have been satisfied, Dawson had higher ambitions.
      别人或许这样就满足了，可道森却有更大的雄心。

写作：”人工智能翻译能否取代人类翻译“

Recent years have seen incredible improvements in the field of artificial intelligence, which has found its way into numerous aspects of our lives. But the most notable aspect is the one of translation. However, AI translation, also known as machine translation, has drawn mixed feelings. Some hail it as a leap forward for human society, enabling people to communicate freely with other cultures by eliminating language barriers, while others fret that it will create misunderstandings and is thus not qualified for translation, or that it will replace human translators, costing them their jobs.

It is modeled on the way the human brain works. Take children for example, who learn language by listening to others and detecting patterns in the language. Similarly, machines are trained by huge volumes of text translated by humans, recognizing patterns in the input data set. When translating, machines will take each word, and use the surrounding context to turn it into a kind of abstract digital representation before trying to find the closest matching representation in the target language, based on what it has learned before. Machine can translate materials at very low to no cost as well as go through a much higher volume of text at a faster rate than humans.

As we start to see the advantages of automatic translation system, we must also be aware of where it might stumble. To begin with, owing to the fact that it translates sentences based on that it has learned, when training texts are not enough, it will make more mistakes. Another limitation involves literature translation, which requires far too supple an understanding of author’s intentions and culture for machines to complete the translation tasks. A third one concerns critical work - technical, financial or medical, say - minor mistakes (of which even the best systems still produce plenty) are unacceptable; a human must stay in charge to vet and edit the output of automatic systems.

As we can see, AI translation is still not perfect and cannot replace human translators so far. Though still not fully-fledged, machine translation dangles the promise of enabling humans to communicate more freely across the globe and translators to do their jobs more quickly.
